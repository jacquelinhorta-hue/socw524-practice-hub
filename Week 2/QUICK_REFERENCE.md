## ‚úÖ DO's and ‚ùå DON'Ts

### Technology Use

#### ‚úÖ DO:
- Use HIPAA-compliant platforms for client communication
- Regularly update passwords and security settings
- Provide clear informed consent about technology use
- Have backup plans when technology fails
- Document technology-related decisions in case notes

#### ‚ùå DON'T:
- Use personal social media to communicate with clients
- Store client information on unsecured devices
- Assume all clients have equal access to technology
- Friend or follow current clients on social media
- Share client information via unencrypted email

### Evidence-Based Practice

#### ‚úÖ DO:
- Stay current with research in your practice area
- Adapt evidence-based interventions for cultural context
- Involve clients in treatment decisions
- Document your clinical reasoning
- Seek consultation when evidence is unclear

#### ‚ùå DON'T:
- Ignore research evidence in favor of personal preference
- Apply interventions rigidly without cultural adaptation
- Make decisions without client input
- Use outdated or discredited interventions
- Practice outside your area of competence

## üîë Key Terms to Know

**Evidence-Based Practice (EBP)**: Integration of best research evidence with clinical expertise and client values

**Algorithmic Bias**: Systematic errors in AI systems that create unfair outcomes for certain groups

**Digital Divide**: Gap between those with access to technology and those without

**Telehealth**: Delivery of health services via telecommunications technology

**HIPAA**: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act - protects patient health information

**Cultural Competence**: Ability to work effectively across cultural differences

**Informed Consent**: Client's agreement to treatment based on full understanding of risks and benefits

**Professional Boundaries**: Limits that protect the therapeutic relationship

**Risk Assessment**: Evaluation of potential harm or danger to clients

**Fidelity**: Adherence to the original design of an intervention
